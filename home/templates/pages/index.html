We are a grass-root non-profit organization striving to ensure that every child regardless of their geographical location has the opportunity to acquire literacy skills, access to books, digital skills,the right to social justice and youth empowerment in order to assist them in their quest to maximize their potential for success. The LGF has a fundamental duty to educate, inspire and provide skill training to young people. This is especially directed at girls in underprivileged communities where this has been dwarfed by poverty and outmoded cultural practices. We as a foundation are committed to the welfare of the youth and children. We believe occurrences such as teenage pregnancy and drug abuse are caused by young people who never had the fortune of a proper education. LGF embraces this responsibility to provide opportunities to all without discrimination on the grounds of their age, culture, nationality, creed, ethnicity, disability, gender, language, racial origin, or religious belief
Our mission: ensuring that every individual in deprived communities has the opportunity to access quality education and training to reach full potential and reduce social vices.

Book Talk and Reading clubs

A Multi-Dimensional Child Poverty analysis in Ghana by UNICEF in 2020 reveals that the vulnerability of children is based on several factors drawn from their different socio-economic, household and geographic characteristics. It reveals that approximately three-in-four children (73.4 per cent) in Ghana are identified as multi-dimensionally poor, facing at least three deprivations at the same time in Ghana

Mentorship Program and Girls' Empowerment Annual Summit

This component of our initiatives is dedicated exclusively to girls, guiding them towards recognizing their potential through career guidance and capacity-building workshops held within our reading clubs. We complement these efforts with an annual summit that brings together stakeholders and guest speakers to empower our beneficiaries in areas such as self-discovery and self-leadership, goal setting and achievement, exploring opportunities for tertiary scholarships and programs, and breaking free from the 'kayaye' culture.

LGF community libraries

Learners Girls Foundation, a dedicated non-governmental organization, has introduced a pioneering library and e-learning center in Paga, situated within the Kassena-Nankana West District of the Upper East Region. This exceptional facility represents a major step in the organization's mission to empower girls and promote education within the community

Digital Inclusion Education program

Digital Innovation Education Program; an innovative virtual-hardware based solution which is designing and looking for funding or partnerships to make it possible. DIEP is seeking to use of a child friendly offline-online learning software with a bilingual language design. The solution involves the creation of digital classrooms in schools with a reliable and sustainable digital learner-friendly educational software which will provide learners with 24 hours learning and teaching materials with a bilingual language learning initiative.
